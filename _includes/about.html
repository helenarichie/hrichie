Cholla

Cholla (Computational Hydrodynamics on paraLLel Architectures) is a GPU-based hydrodynamics code developed by Evan Schneider. Cholla was designed to be massively-parallel and extremely efficient, and has been run on some of the world's largest supercomputers. Check out Cholla's GitHub and Cholla's simulation gallery to learn more!

STEPUP

I served as the Lead Undergraduate of the Survey of Transiting Extrasolar Planets at the University of Pittsburgh (STEPUP) from 2017-2020. STEPUP is an undergraduate research group that uses transit photometry to detect exoplanets, conducting observations using the 16" Keeler Telescope based out of the Allegheny Observatory.

SIA

STEPUP Image Analysis (SIA) is a tool for easy photometric analysis that I developed in 2017. SIA was designed to extract light curves from astronomical image data and is being used by the STEPUP team at Pitt to study transiting exoplanets. SIA is also being used by students at the Allegheny Observatory. Check out SIA's GitHub to learn more!
